The Cooperators - Work Terms 1 & 2 (COOP 1000 & COOP 2000)

Technical Support Analyst

From May 2019 - December 2019

Information About the Employer

The Co-operators are a cooperative insurance company consisting of: The Co-operators Group Limited, Co-operators Financial Services Limited, Co-operators General Insurance Company, Co-operators Life Insurance Company, CUMIS, The CUMIS group limited, The Edge Benefits, Addenda Capital Inc., The Sovereign General Insurance Company, The Premier group of companies, Technicost, Federated Agencies Limited, and HB Group Insurance Management Ltd. and COSECO Insurance Company. I worked at the head office located in downtown Guelph, ON. The Co-operators offers a diverse workplace atmosphere, your coworkers include all types of people with all sorts of educational backgrounds. The Co-operators includes graphic designers, software developers, insurance advisors, and pretty much everything in between.

Insurance is not inherently a computer science related field, but many aspects of computer science are used in the company to increase productivity. The company uses things like robotic process automation to increase productivity, however, my role as a Technical Support Analyst gave me a unique insight into the field of Computer Science. Through this role I was able to see what end users often struggled with when they were using software and where software tends to fail. This viewpoint is very valuable for a Software Development team, as often times developers will have varying degrees of bias when designing software. A developer can often make assumptions about their end users that might not be entirely true. A developer might think that their application is user friendly because they know how it works, but, they were the ones working on the software, so there knowledge will far surpass that of the majority of end users that will be using the software. Being a Technical Support Analyst at an insurance company, I gained a valuable perspective on Software Design.

Job Description

I worked as a Technical Support Analyst at The Co-operators from May 2019 – December 2019. In this role, I provided technical support to Co-operators employees who were having a wide variety of technical issues. Issues ranged anywhere from laptops not turning on to potential viruses. Through my experiences in this role, I gained many valuable skills and learned many important lessons.

Communication, customer service, and general technical knowledge are assets for this role, as your main goal is to aid in client service and provide competent and coherent support to people who can have any degree of technical knowledge.

The dreams of yesterday are the hopes of today and the reality of tomorrow. Science has not yet mastered prophecy. We predict too much for the next year and yet far too little for the next ten.

General technical knowledge came easy to me as the Software Engineering program is very technical, and so I was made previously aware of most of the common software that is used for productivity. However, companies often have proprietary software that is built to the specific needs of the company. I needed to learn the software and technologies as I went throughout the role in order to be able to provide an adequate level of support.

Before coming into this role, I knew very little about the physical components of a computer. This role allowed me to develop my knowledge of the inner workings of a computer’s hardware. As a Software Engineering student, we typically focus on software topics and design, not so much practical hardware.
